Welcome To Vision for Ewell

Hello and welcome to Vision for Ewell, a website for the community group of residents who live in the town of Ewell in Surrey. Within this website, you will find many things related to the village of Ewell; an example of this is local events occurring around Ewell. Also, on this website, you will find helpful resources such as local transport connections in Ewell. Furthermore, you can also find out about the history of Ewell and its place within modern London and the United Kingdom.

Ewell Village Fair

The Ewell Village Fair is a time that unites the whole village of Ewell. It occurs once a year and is organised by the local church. At the fair, you can experience the best that the town has to offer: local artisans display their work for sale, local chefs produce wonderful different bites for your enjoyment, and there are plenty of activities for children. History of Ewell village

The village is situated on the spring line between the North Downs chalk and London Clay, with sources that supply the Hogsmill River. Ewell is located in the northeast part of Surrey, not far from London, and is now part of the Borough of Epsom and Ewell. In Tudor times, Ewell ended up losing some of its lands when Henry VIII had Nonsuch Palace built in the nearby parish of Cuddington. Nonsuch began construction in 1538. It was constructed in 1556 by the Earl of Arundel and sold to Elizabeth I in 1592 through his son-in-law, Lord Lumley. Ewell Jail

In the village of Ewell, down a small street, a small jail can be found. It is so small that it would not even be considered a jail. The jail is estimated to have been built in 1777. Although its primary purpose was to hold criminals, it also served as a store for the local fire extinguishing equipment for the local area. The jail building is made of bricks, which was done to ensure that prisoners could not escape. Click the link below to find out more about Ewell Jail.

Nonsuch Park

Nonsuch Park is a park that is located near Ewell village. In the north of Ewell village, there are many entrances to the park. The park has plenty of car parking spaces, so people can travel to the park in their families' cars. The park contains paved and unpaved sections for various activities such as football or cricket. Bourne Hall

In the centre of historic Ewell Village in Surrey, Bourne Hall looks down on the headwaters of the Hogsmill River. The adjacent natural gardens and lake provide an ideal, peaceful, secluded village setting for any social or business function. This magnificent modern structure with unique architectural interest has been open since 1970. Bourne Hall strives to provide the perfect venue for you with a variety of different-sized halls and rooms, exceptional facilities, easy and convenient transportation links, and an experienced venue staff. Train connections

Ewell is home to two significant stations with connections that link the village to London. The first station is Ewell West. This station connects the town of Ewell to Guilford and Dorking in Surrey to London Waterloo in Central London. The trains at Ewell west are served by south western railway and are also in the oyster zone. The next station that is located within Ewell is Ewell East. This station links Ewell to Dorking, Horsham and Epsom to London Victoria and London Bridge in central London. Trains from Ewell east are run by southern rail, and the station is also in the London oyster zone. Click the link below to find out more about the stations in Ewell.

Bus Connections

Ewell is connected to many other locations in London via the TFL bus network; the TFL bus network allows for fast, safe and quick transport around London. Both stations in Ewell are linked via buses, which means that residents of Ewell can get from the train station to near their homes in comfort. Within Ewell, there are frequent bus connections to nearby Epsom that allow residents to visit whenever they need to quickly. Buses also go to Sutton, which has many critical infrastructures such as banks. For more information about buses around Ewell, follow the link below.

Road Connections

Ewell has many major road connections to the surrounding areas and to many long-distance locations. Although no motorways pass through the town of Ewell there are many A-roads that pass Ewell. One road that passes through the town of Ewell is the A240. The A240 links the town of Epsom to Kingston. These road connections allow for easy access to the town of Kingston which then allows drivers to connect to future destinations such as Heathrow Airport.
